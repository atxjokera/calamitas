Dante[2]

Dante, whose original name is Gaspar,[3] is one of the protagonists of the series Paranormal Order, present in Deconjuration, Calamity and Signals from the Other Side.

He was an occultist who was arrested for his participation in a ritual involving Luciano Carvalho.[4] Later, due to his help during the investigation of Elizabeth Webber's disappearance case, he became a full agent of the Ordo Realitas.

After surviving the quest involving the Deconjuration and Kian, he returns in Calamity, joining the team responsible for the search for the Relics of Calamity, being one of the Markeds of the Ordo Realitas.

Appearance[]

Childhood[]

As a child, Dante had long blonde hair halfway down his back and light blue eyes.

Dante wore a white T-shirt with 3 stripes on the bottom, with brown pants and dark shoes, on his wrists he had 2 wooden bracelets.[5]

Deconjuration[]

Dante is a 27-year-old man with pale skin and light eyes. He wears square black glasses and has a Sigil of Knowledge tattooed on his forehead that resembles the infinity symbol. He has a huge scar on his chest from heart surgery performed in his childhood.[6] He has long blonde hair with strands of his bangs tied to the back of his head and a braid on either side of his face, both held in place by the details that make up his necklace. Dante wears a gray dress shirt with a clerical collar and string resembling a rosary, and a black jacket.[7] His rosary has a reliquary in which he keeps a lock of Jasmin Cristal's hair next to one of Beatrice Portinari.[8]

Both of Dante's hands are covered in different symbols. On the back of her right hand, are recognizable and present in our world: the spiral of the Golden Ratio, a Compass Rose and the mathematical formula for mass-energy equivalence.[9] On his palm, Dante has the Decadenza Ritual Symbol and, on his fingers, it is possible to see: on the indicator, a sequence of numbers; in the middle, a chain of DNA; on the ring, a representation of the solar system; and at least one Rutherford's atomic model. His left hand is covered in Sigils of Knowledge and has the Death Symbol, tattooed both on the back of his hand and on his ring finger, while on the palm of his hand he has the tattoo of the Cicatrization Ritual Symbol. On the arm region, Dante tattooed the Symbol of Temporal Destruction Ritual.

During the events of the 12th episode of Deconjuration, Dante makes a drastic change in his look: he cuts his hair with the scissors from his medical kit, leaving only a slightly longer lock; he stops wearing his glasses — as he had his myopia cured by the paranormal after his paranormal exposure reached a considerable level —; and leaves his old coat with Beatrice and is left with only his priest cleric unbuttoned and his shirt sleeves rolled up, revealing his forearms which are completely covered in symbols and Sigils of Knowledge.[10]

In the 15th episode of Deconjuration, Dante uses his Psychic Read Ritual on Beatrice in her form of Existed. Upon entering the agent's mind, Dante receives one of the most disturbing and macabre visions he has ever experienced, discovering the hell that the woman and all other Existeds Ones are going through.[11] This experience generates such a shock that he faints and, when he wakes up and opens his eyes, he realizes he can't see anything: the trauma was so great which took his vision and his eyes turned white.[12]

With the emotional upheaval for Dante, in the events of the 16th episode of Deconjuration, the occultist decides to take some time to rest and think and returns to St. Menefreda Orphanage with the help of Ivete. Walking around, he finds a piece of white cloth and ties it over his eyes to hide his newly acquired blindness.[13] Now, take it with you a copy of the book Divine Comedy, in which several of its rituals are marked in thick black ink.[14] To use his skills, Dante just needs to hold the necessary ingredients, place his hand on top of the desired ritual, concentrate on the symbol and thus activate it.

Calamity[]

A few months after the events of Deconjuration, Dante grows his hair out and uses a bun to keep part of his hair tied up. He also wears a black short-sleeved t-shirt, brownish pants with chains attached to his waist wrapped with a small storage bag and, on his bare feet, he has white bands that go up to his calf. He no longer has a blindfold, keeping his eyes constantly closed, accompanied on his head by a long white hood that goes down to his legs, secured by a brooch in the shape of his brand. It is also possible to notice that the tattoos he previously had are no longer visible.[15]

Upon opening his eyes, Dante presents changes in his form, resulting from his affinity with the element of Death: his tattoos reappear, his eyes become completely black and two stripes appear, one that runs across his right eye and the other horizontally across his lip, and his ears become slightly pointed — an overall appearance quite similar to that of a Lusidii.[16]

Signals from the Other Side[]

In his brief appearance, Dante still has his blonde hair, now with two locks at the front in the shape of an M, and his tattoos on his body. He also appears to have a thin beard and mustache, as well as wearing a very elegant blue suit over a white turtleneck shirt and a blue vest. He has a black brooch with yellow details attached to his blue suit that looks very similar to the rosary he wore in the past.[17]

Personality[]

Deconjuration[]

Dante was initially monotonous and almost always distant, avoiding showing strong emotions, both positive and negative. Still, he seemed polite, accommodating and helpful. Dante understands his situation, not demanding the trust of others even though he is always willing to help with whatever he can.

The only time Dante loses his composure and calm appearance is when his childhood friend Lilian is in danger, as he sees her as his younger sister and tries to protect her at any cost. This is exemplified when the two meet again at the 4th episode of Deconjuration: when he realizes that his sister doesn't remember his real name, he decides to pretend not to know her.

After being unable to stop or reverse his sister's transformation into the 11th episode of Deconjuration, Dante goes through an emotional avalanche,[18] which causes him to change drastically, adopting a more focused and assertive stance, avoiding questions about his sudden change and current state, as he deems them as not relevant to the progress of the investigation.

Calamity[]

1 year after the events of the Final Day of Deconjuration, Dante appears quite serious in Paranormal Order: Calamity, his speech generally being in a low and constant tone, similar to the way he spoke when he met the agents of the D-Force. Even so, Dante talks about the mission normally, not being as quiet as he was initially. He is also very concerned and helpful with his fellow agents. Dante, however, is also less tolerant of forgiveness,[19] losing control and raising your voice in some arguments more than usual.

Skills[]

  • Occultism: Dante knows several different magics and creatures due to the contact he has had with the occult since he was a child. He has experience with various rituals and is seen from the beginning to be able to transcend on his own.
  • Medicine: Being a doctor, Dante knows techniques for treating injuries and illnesses.

Biography[]

Past[]

Gaspar was born between 1992 and 1993[20] on an unknown date and grew up isolated from the other children at St. Menefreda Orphanage until 2006[21] due to his fragile health caused by his heart problems. During his childhood, he had to undergo a very expensive surgery due to aortic valve disease, and if it weren't for the help of Arnaldo Fritz, he wouldn't have gotten enough money to have this surgery.[6]

After a while, Dante started to hang out with the other children, but only made one friend, Lilian. The two together used to spend their afternoons reading "The Divine Comedy" on a hill near the orphanage while Beatrice braided his hair.[22] At a certain point, Arnaldo names Gaspar and Lilian as Beatrice and Dante, a name that the two have carried with them throughout their lives.

Sometime after his friend Beatrice was adopted on February 11, 2009,[23] during a supposedly calm night, The Host causes a fire at the orphanage, where several children ended up dying from the flames that spread through the place.[24] During this fire, Dante was awakened by Jasmin Cristal to run away from there. The two, while fleeing, found Leonardo Gomes under rubble. They did not hesitate to save him from the fire, taking the boy along with them.[25] After this, Dante moved away from most of the other children in the orphanage, except for Jasmin and Leonardo. The three begin to live in the streets and[26] then they start sharing a studio apartment in São Paulo. During the time they lived together, his partner Jasmin was stabbed to death,[27] which led Dante to become an occultist to try and bring her back somehow.

In January 2020, Dante, manipulated by Leonardo, engages in a sequence of occult rituals to, as proposed by Leonardo to Dante, save the life of a child, which ends up going very wrong in Dante's view.[28] What actually happened in these rituals is that Dante performed Kian's Deconjuration. Leonardo had made a plan that would involve manipulating both Dante and Luciano Carvalho. Dante was told that to save the child they would need to use several rituals and two volunteers to give part of their life to the boy. Luciano, when manipulated by Kian, kidnaps Denis Batista, a terminally ill child that Kian intended to use as a source of Fear, and her husband, Fernando Carvalho. Upon arrival, Dante then performs an order of rituals: First, the Knowledge Passage Ritual, which caused Fernando's mind to enter Luciano's body; then another that split Kian's mind and knowledge, sending the knowledge to the empty shell that remained of Fernando's body and his mind to Luciano; again the ritual of passing on knowledge, which caused Denis's mind to enter Leonardo Gomes' body, leaving the boy's body empty. Due to the fragile veil of the place, this resulted in the formation of a blood creature — something planned by Kian to fake a failure in the ritual. He promptly killed Denis in Leonardo's body and fled the scene, leaving Dante to think that the supposed ritual to save the child had gone wrong. The rituals ended up resulting in the death of Denis, the joining of Fernando with Luciano and the supposed death of his friend.[4] Dante was found with his friend's body in his arms and taken into custody by two Ordo Realitas agents, therefore kept in a prison cell.[27]

Deconjuration[]

After spending months in his cell at the Ordo Realitas base,[7] Dante is reunited with Lilian, who grew up with him at the St. Menefreda Orphanage, whose name now is Beatrice. However, when he finds her, he pretends not to remember her. He is temporarily released to help Beatrice's team with the progression of their investigation. Soon after, he discovers that one of the volunteers for the ritual and one of the agents who had arrested him were part of the same team and refused to trust him because of his past. Even so, the decision is maintained.

The group finds the orphanage where Elizabeth was being held thanks to Dante's information. Upon finding her, Liz recognizes Dante by name as one of the occultists involved in the Leonardo Gomes case. Liz treats Dante harshly and intimidatingly, asking the others about how Verity had allowed a "worm" like him to leave his cell. Dante is succinct in his words to Liz and does not respond to her provocations, remaining passive. When escaping, the group faces several monsters, including the Degolified. This creature with the body of a girl talks to Dante through a ritual and identifies herself as Nina, a little girl who had grown up with Dante and Beatrice in the orphanage. She says that she was murdered the day of the fire and that she wanted one of her killers' bunny to let them go. The team delivers the rabbit to the creature, but after an intense chase. At the end of the battle, the creature reveals that Nubi, Henri and Gal had killed it on the day of the fire.[29] After this, only the rabbit remains and Dante realizes that a Death ritual was installed in him and decides to transcend.

At the door of the Orphanage, the group is blocked by Escribes, the occultists responsible for kidnapping Liz. Next to them, there was Leonardo Gomes chained up, wearing a hood and repeating the same word the whole time: Kian. [30] Dante is irritated by the situation of his former best friend and says he will save him. During the encounter, the agents are easily stopped by the antagonists, who are clearly much stronger than them. With everyone paralyzed, Gal murders Elizabeth with Joui's katana in front of everyone. The occultists lock everyone in the orphanage. The group is eventually freed by Erin and Fernando. Dante goes down with Joui to the orphanage courtyard and helps him analyze his katana, now full of spirals, identifying it as a Death ritual. The group recognizes their weakness before the Escribes and, with Dante's help, everyone transcends on the spot.

After Kaiser's recommendations, the group continues their investigation to the Devilish Mansion. There, they discover that this was the house where Leonardo Gomes was adopted for only a few months before returning to the Orphanage. The team then investigates the mansion and faces various entities. The first one was the Traveler, who altered Kaiser's photos, erasing their faces. After its first appearance, the creature killed Tristan and made Kaiser lose his memory of those in the photos. The battle against the Traveler, in addition to killing two police officers, turned Beatrice into an Existed after Orpheu was killed.[31] Seeing his considerate sister become an Existed caused drastic changes in Dante. His suffering was so great that he was able to create a paranormal object that provided him a Psychic Read Ritual. Furthermore, he cut off all his hair, keeping only a single strand in the front representing Beatrice, as a promise that he would bring her back.[10] After attacks from Blood Zombies, Beatrice's life is in danger, but the group manages to save her and decide to keep her in the van for safety.

The second creature was The Lurker, which was tormenting Kaiser, Arthur and Ivete, it was discovered that the cat, Jennifer, was marked by the entity, which had been watching the three of them ever since. The fight against the Lurker was intense, however, Dante stays behind and doesn't get hurt too much, in this battle, Dante helps Joui, who was extremely injured during the fight, preventing the young man from dying from a gunshot to the chest, after the confrontation, everyone returns to base and, as a last resort, Dante decides to try to read Beatrice's mind to see if it was still possible to save her, he performs the Psychic Read Ritual, but all he receives are agonized screams from several people, including Beatrice, they scream and ask to be observed and remembered. Dante feels severe pain in his head, causing him to scream and cry in pain, collapsing at the end of the ritual. Sometime later, he wakes up on the infirmary's stretcher with Joui by his side, however, Dante can't see anything.[12] Not believing in his sudden blindness, Dante argues with Joui who calms him down afterwards. Dante comes to the conclusion that it is impossible to save Beatrice and asks the group to take them to the orphanage to put an end to the girl's suffering. Once there, Dante asks Joui for help to aim at Beatrice's head and give her a mercy shot. Dante pulls the trigger and buries his sister next to the tree where they read the Divine Comedy together. Dante sits next to the tomb and spends 4 hours straight reciting the verses from the book until he eventually falls asleep. After a while, Dante decides to spend time at the orphanage to reflect and the group returns to the mansion without him.

After the group faces the Listeneds, Dante is left at the mansion with the team by Ivete. Next, the team battles the Drowned Woman. Dante is quite lost in the confrontation, not knowing how to deal with his recent blindness very well. After finishing at the mansion, they find out about a former resident still alive: Frederico Mare, and go to interrogate him. With Fred's report, they find out about Álvaro Augusto and his connection with Leonardo Gomes and Deconjuration.

Everyone heads to the cemetery at Joui's request, who had been acting strange all day. The Sect of the Masks appears and Joui reveals that he made a deal with the sect to protect the team and defeat Kian and the Escribes. Joui's departure enrages Dante, who claims that the man had promised to help him kill Gal and was now breaking his promise. Joui retorts by saying that as long as he was standing, his promise would be standing as well. Everyone then returns to base and the team meets to analyze the documents. Eventually, Arthur receives a call from the neighboring children of the Devilish Mansion, who say there is something wrong going on. Because they had become attached to the children and were worried, the group rushed to the location, finding Henri and Enpap-X. A brutal confrontation occurs, in which Kaiser and Erin are extremely injured. Everyone turns their attention to Erin and with that, Henri approaches and takes Kaiser hostage with the intention of killing him. At the time of the execution, Bruno appears and convinces Henri to let him kill his former friend. Bruno then surprises everyone by attacking Henri unnoticed and helping Kaiser to heal and get up. Dante is confused by what happened, only understanding after an explanation from Arthur. Bruno explains that the last day of the Deconjuration had arrived. They recover, but Henri gets up in surprise. A battle occurs and Henri, injured, uses a Blood Link Ritual to link the lives of Arthur, Kaiser and Luciano to him. Threatening to kill himself and take them all with him, the agents allow him to escape. The team gets together and goes to the Mansion, entering the sewer, where the children are. In the Corridors of this sewer, they face Sukkalgir, a creature originating from the body of the nun Thelma from the orphanage. Dante delivers the final blow to the specter, revealing that he had never liked the woman.

At the end of this battle, Dante and the other agents return to the St. Menefreda Orphanage, where they find Gal, Kian and Tim, as well as the bodies of Beatrice, Orpheu, Tristan and Elizabeth hanging from a tree. The D-Force team battles Gal, who proves to be quite agile and powerful. Dante witnesses the death of Erin, who explodes after being fatally attacked by the occultist, as well as the deaths of Alexia and Samantha, two of the agents who came for reinforcements along with Aaron and Verity. During the fight, Gal had drawn a transcending symbol on the ground, causing Luciano to transcend, causing him to become Kian, who had been hidden in his body all this time. Kian reveals his plan to end all paranormal by murdering everyone who has knowledge of it. He reveals to Dante that from the beginning he was Leonardo Gomes and that he had manipulated him into performing the Deconjuration. He even confesses that he killed Dante's pregnant wife just to make his plan work.[32] Dante despairs, realizing that he had been a pawn in Kian's hands. In the midst of so much blood, death and chaos, Dante is rescued by the Sect of the Masks thanks to the Cineraria Ritual performed by Kaiser. He survives along with Arthur, being both transported back to the Ordo Realitas Base, with the weight of the loss of Kaiser, Erin, Fernando and Luciano plaguing them. Mr. Verity, even if shaken, consoles them and motivates them to continue fighting to honor those who are gone, even if this is an arduous path and often seems impossible. Dante, even without faith, accepts the leader's comfort and asks for his help to carry out his revenge against Kian.

Timeskip[]

Dante, after hearing Arthur's stories about his old cases, became curious about Carpazinha's case and decided to go and investigate the place alone. He asked Mr. Verity for documents about the case and then he traveled to Carpazinha in search of what he knew as Holy Cradle. Arriving in the city, he asked several of the locals for clues about his investigation, but was unable to find anything that would help him. However, as he approached the forest, Dante felt something he had never felt before, a terrible chill. Something seemed to call him and Dante was completely terrified, unable to enter the forest.[33] He eventually returned to São Paulo and didn't tell Arthur or Ivete about his visit to their hometown. Arthur only found out about this in 9th episode of Calamity, after Dante told the group inside the ruins of Ordo Calamitas.

Calamity[]

Months after the events of Dsconjuration, Dante and Arthur are assigned by Mr. Verity to work with a new team to investigate the appearance of a "Devil" holding hands with a child in the interior of Mato Grosso. While they discuss the mission, Verity says that the current case has many similarities with one of Arnaldo Fritz's old missions and that this would be the Order's best clue to discover more information about Arnaldo Fritz's Grimoire and the whereabouts of the Relics of Calamity.

After a long journey, Dante and Arthur meet with Carina Leone, Rubens Naluti and Antônio Pontevedra, at the Gominho's house, where the supposed appearance of the creature had been recorded, heading towards Elza Verlaz's house. Investigating the place, the Vultures team discovers that Elza had met and become pregnant by a man from the circus, who had convinced her to use his son in an attempt to summon the Devil, until Arnaldo appeared and saved Eusébio, who had gone to the house in day of the ritual, thus preventing the invocation of the Devil. Furthermore, they discover that there was a supposed trapdoor in Elza's house and decide to investigate closely. When they are inside, the Gominhos and the police officer Felipe appear at the house looking for them. The team asks them to leave, because it could be dangerous, but before that, a knock on the door is heard along with someone calling "Mommy?". Cícero says that it was the Devil who was at the door and says that making a pact with him was the only way to win in life. He opens the door and Dante comes across the body of Denis Batista, the boy he tried to save in the ritual in which he was manipulated into making the Deconjuration Order[34] Dante is extremely shaken, but pulls himself together for the fight. During the confrontation, he is worried about the injuries of the others, since that creature only existed because of him. After the fight, they find a page of Arnaldo's Grimoire and then leave the city.

Returning to São Paulo, four days have passed and everyone is at the base of Ape Hangers. During this time, Dante and Agatha tried to acquire new information by analyzing the page. Dante meets with the rest of his team to report their mission to Mr. Verity, and after a long conversation, they discover that when adding the page to the grimoire, new pages appear. One of them hid a clue to the location of documents, a password to a safe in the Leone's Mansion, in Italy. With the preparations ready, the team, together with Clarissa, leaves for Rome, and during the trip, Dante talks to Arthur and asks him about how much he knew about Arnaldo, he briefly tells him about what he knew and Dante tells him that in order for them to face the challenges that lie ahead, they first needed to face the demons of the past.

Rituals and Abilities[]

Rituals[]

Deconjuration[]

Third Eye Ritual: By using this ritual, Dante has the ability to detect paranormal activities in his field of vision, such as cursed objects, monsters, and hauntings.[37]

Decadence Ritual "Decadenza": Dante, by using this ritual, is able to create a black smoke from his hand, which, upon contact with the target, causes ut to contract and accelerate a disease in its body, ranging from bubbles around the skin to vomiting with blood and its internal organs.[38]

Cicatrization Ritual "Paradiso": Dante, in using this ritual, has the power to create a black smoke from his hand, which flies in a spiral shape towards an open wound. Upon contact, the wound begins to age rapidly in a spiral shape, which can heal or infect it if it is not clean.[39]

Time Destruction Ritual "Rotura": After transcending, Dante learns a new destruction ritual. By focusing on the symbol, Dante can cause his target to rot and disintegrate. Like the Decadence Ritual and the Cicatrization Ritual, the Ritual of Destruction is a "timelapse", but now representing the effects of an autolysis.[40]

Psychic Read Ritual: After transcending again, Dante learns a ritual capable of reading the mind of his target or predicting its intentions.[11]

Melt Blood Creatures Ritual "Brute": Dante, after once again transcending, this time with a book bought from Agatha's Shop, learns the Melt Blood Ritual, which consists in burning the blood of creatures on the other side, being extremely effective against creatures of Blood.[41]

Calamity[]

Sensory Boost Ritual: By cutting off the palm of his hand, Dante’s eyes turn red.[42] After that, for a short period of time, he gains more ease in actions involving perception, investigate, marksmanship and melee.[43]

Shuffle Ritual "Trinitá": By squeezing two batteries together, Dante, in the shape of a black figure distorts in time, being able to create up to four copies of himself, making those around him don't know which of these is not a clone.[44] Using an enhancement of this same ritual, Dante makes his clones act independently, in addition being able to swap their position with any of the existing copies.[45]

Spiral Echo Ritual "Eco Dolor": Levitating both hands over each other in front of the face, with the palms facing each other, when blowing, creates a black silhouette of the target, causing it to have its movements repeated by a kind of figure.[46] After preparation, crushing the modeled creature and clapping hands, all attacks given to the enemy are "replicated" again in shape of a figure.[47]

Cicatrization Ritual "Paradiso": Dante, upon realizing the state of Carina in the fight against The Needy, activates a ritual, blowing a black spiral fog that begins to go towards Carina, accelerating the healing of her wounds. By using this ritual, Dante can accelerate the healing of wounds, healing 2d8 + 2, being able to spend two more EPs and improve this ritual, gaining more 1d8 + 2 every two EP spent.[48]

Mental Dive Ritual "Inferno": By piercing the five fingers of his hand with a needle and putting his hand on the forehead of an individual, both the target and Dante remains in a kind of trance, being taken mentally to a totally black and empty place, called "Hell", where one can question the person, forced to answer your questions with yes or no.[49] Through this same ritual, Dante is able to project his memories to his target, being able to choose which ones will be highlighted.[50]

Decadence Ritual "Decadenza": Dante, by using this ritual, is able to create a black smoke that comes out of his mouth, going towards his target and if it comes into contact with him, causes him to suffer a process of extremely accelerated and deadly degradation.[51]

Paradox Ritual: Upon seeing the approach of a horde of Blood Zombies summoned by The Devil itself, Dante with his hand makes a spiral movement in the air and a black fog comes out of his fingers, creating a large spiral of the same color on the floor, under the blood creatures that begin to wither and rot, killing several of them.[52]

Creature Summoning Ritual: Upon realizing the critical situation during the fight against The Host, Dante begins to expel large amounts of a Black Ooze from his mouth, nose and eyes, forming a large puddle in front of him, while a skeletal figure, The Puppet, starts to crawl out of his body.[53]

Ooze Tentacles Ritual: When conjuring this ritual, Dante puts his hand on the ground and makes black tentacles come out of the ground, in a small area, those who hold everyone close and if they keep the person trapped, compress it.[54]

Focused Energy Shot Ritual: Focusing on the symbol and using an electronic device, the user fires a projectile of focused Energy towards a target.[55]

Abilities[]

Deconjuration[]

Accessible Ingredients: When transcending, Dante passed one of the paranormal’s barriers, which allows him to choose an archetype, and, since he had no cursed item, he can also choose an skill. Thus, he chose Accessibel Ingredients, which allows the user to quickly loot ingredients as a free action in combat.[56] This skill also allows Dante to loot his improvised grimoire in a practical way, as the book has become a key ingredient for performing his rituals.[57]

Sanity Advantage: By reaching 20 Power points on his character sheet, Dante gains an advantage in all his sanity tests, finding a creature or using a ritual, in addition to not anymore fail a Power test except when he gets a disaster roll.[58]

Unbreakable Focus: By transcending with a cursed item, being an advanced level Occultist, Dante was able to learn a skill beyond the ritual he would already learn. Thus, he chose Unbreakable Focus, which allows the user to conjure two rituals at once, requiring a greater concentration during the process, making it impossible to move during use. This ability also allows the user to continue focusing on an already active ritual while using another.[59]

Paranormal Defense: By transcending after the fight against Tirigan and Sukkalgir, Dante acquires two new abilities, one of them being the Paranormal Defense, which allows Dante to give a disadvantage to an attack targeted to him by spending 3 Occultism points.[60]

Other Side Affinity: By transcending after the fight against Tirigan and Sukkalgir, Dante acquires two new abilities, one of them being the Other Side Affinity, that speeds up his Occultism point recovery by 1, now recovering two points per hour.[60]

Calamity[]

Affinity with the Element of the Other Side: Dante has affinity with the Element of Death, allowing him to cast Death rituals without requiring ingredients, and he can also use Death rituals as a standard action, rather than a full action. In addition, he can develop a certain affinity with Death’s paranormal powers.

Quick Ritual: Using 3 EP increases the range of a ritual (from touch to short, short to medium or medium to long).[61]

Marked by Death: Dante has advantage in tests of resistance to Death rituals and attacks ensuring an extra die.[62]

Defensive Tactics: The user can use Intellect instead of Agility on Reflex rolls.[63]

Exorcism: The user can use an occultism die roll as therapy along with a presence test to get someone out of the insane state.[64]

Paranormal Strength: Dante’s rituals that deal damage or heal receive an additional bonus equivalent to his user’s Intellect, which in Dante’s case is 3.

Relationships[]

  • Agatha Volkomenn: Dante and Agatha began to work together after the events of Deconjuration. Dante has been concerned about the young woman’s well-being, and Agatha tries to impress the occultist through her knowledge of the occult and seems to admire him as a mentor. During the events of the war at the colosseum it's possible to notice how much Dante cares about Agatha and her safety, showing himself completely distressed for not having protected her as soon as possible. The young woman also seems to call for him when she was having nightmares, shortly after being injured by Kian’s Blade of Fear, showing how Dante was a supporting figure to her.[74] The particular concern of the occultist for the girl is always very evident, since even though he was exhausted after the battle against Kian, he still thought about her well-being and how to help her. After returning to Brazil, Dante spends a lot of time observing the state of the girl, seeking a way to treat her.
  • Antônio Pontevedra: Dante treats Balu very well the moment they meet. Even realizing that he was not very comfortable with the use of rituals, Dante always tries to help him with his healing skills as best he can, which shows that he cares about his safety. During the events that occurred in the Leone's Mansion, Dante ends up creating a distrust in Balu because of the little information he had about him. However, after the events where Dante discovers who was the true traitor in the Order, Dante erases all his distrust with Antônio, and with this, he begins to completely trust him as his ally.
  • Arthur Cervero: Dante and Arthur were polite to each other when they met. Because they were the only survivors of the team after the Final Day of the Deconjuration, Dante and Arthur get close enough, being constantly together and caring a lot about each other’s safety and well-being. In addition, Dante worries about Arthur’s life, as they formed a strong bond of friendship, taking care of him as much as they can and supporting him in difficult times.
  • Beatrice Portinari: Dante and Beatrice were very close friends during childhood. When they finally reunited, however, he pretended not to recognize her and proceeded to try to find out what happened to her memories. However, it was apparent that her opinion was important to him more than the rest of the group, and protecting her has always been his priority. Dante was the one who buried Beatrice and, since then, is very shaken by the loss of his childhood best friend, whom he considered as a younger sister.
  • Carina Leone: Although he knows her for a short time, Dante constantly demonstrates his concern for Carina. During the events of the Leone's Mansion, Dante several times tries to comfort her in the face of the tragic situation of the girl. Amid the symbolic tribute to the Famiglia Leone, Dante supports Carina saying that everyone around her would help the young woman deal with her pain, showing how he sympathizes with her situation. After all these events, Carina ends up acquiring a lot of confidence in Dante, with both protecting themselves as team partners. Dante always treats Carina with kindness, offering to help her several times when he needs to.
  • Clarissa Leão: Although not very close, Clarissa was one of the only survivors of the Final Day of the Deconjuration along with Dante, and so the two ended up sharing a common desire to avenge their friends. Dante trusts Clarissa and cares about her safety. It is possible to realize that he always tries to help her when he can, whether in the middle of a fight or out of it. Due to common motivation, Dante wishes that she also achieves her goal. After finding out about the woman’s betrayal against the Order, Dante laments the situation and states that they needed to find her.
  • Elizabeth Webber: Liz was hostile towards Dante, but upon seeing how she protected Beatrice, she opened up to him about her past, showing that she had put her trust in him. Dante always acted with respect towards Liz, attending her funeral.
  • Erin Parker: Erin did not trust Dante, and the man understood the reason. However, in the final episodes of Deconjuration, the relationship between them was more softened; even if they disagreed from time to time, the two were teammates and cared for each other.
  • Fernando Carvalho: Dante was present in the ritual of Luciano and Fernando, so Fernando was suspicious of all his attitudes. However, Dante had no negative feelings toward him, wanting to help him with his injuries. In the final episodes of the third season, it was shown that the two had built a fellowship by being on the same team.
  • Gal: Dante and Gal were colleagues at the St. Menefreda Orphanage during childhood. After the events of the 6th episode of Deconjuration, Dante is angered by Gal, especially for finding him guilty of his fellow orphans entering the occultism. Even with all the feeling of hatred, Dante still sees Gal as a lost child, just as he was abandoned and manipulated by Kian, so that during the debate proposed by The Host about saving or not Gal from death, Dante was merciful and chose to try to save him, even though some of his companions disapproved of the decision.
  • Jasmin Cristal: Dante, Jasmin and Leonardo began together in the occultism and even shared the same house for a while. Jasmin allegedly was stabbed to death in an unknown context, only to be revealed that Leo and Dante were at the scene and that the young woman died in Dante’s arms. Dante is very sorry for the loss of Jasmin, having as main goal to try to bring the girl back. After the events of the 12th episode of Deconjuration, Dante receives confirmation that it is impossible to bring Jasmin back, which causes him to cut her hair, leaving only a lock to symbolize that Beatrice would be all that was left for him and that he had lost Jasmin forever.[10] During the 20th episode of Deconjuration, it is revealed that their relationship was loving,[32] and that Jasmin was killed by Kian while pregnant to motivate Dante to become an occultist in hopes of bringing her back.[75]
  • Joui Jouki: Joui was very hostile towards Dante from the beginning, but he never showed anger or complained about it. After all the events at the Orphanage, Dante decides he wants to defeat Gal and stop the Escribes. Realizing Dante’s motivations and acknowledging his error of judgment, Joui accepts him as an investigation partner, thus starting a partnership with the same goal. In the course of the Deconjuration events, with Joui being more likely to help Dante, mainly because of Gaspar’s sudden blindness, the two maintain a neutral relationship between teammates. However, after Joui’s return after a year of events involving Kian and the Deconjuration, it is evident that now Dante and Joui have a stronger bond of friendship, helping each other and cherishing each other’s safety. Similarly, Joui’s death in the battle against Kian shakes Dante strongly, showing that he considered him a good person. Dante carries Joui’s memory in his mind and heart and says that the friend would continue living through him.
  • Kaiser: Kaiser was never angered by Dante’s presence, stating his usefulness to the investigation, and relied on his abilities and instincts. At first, Kaiser came to distrust Dante and accuse him of being responsible for tormenting him with the faceless photographs, but that was soon put aside. After the events in the Devilish Mansion, even after some disagreements, the two became closer, especially after Kaiser used a ritual to make him see again and then give him a photo he had taken of Beatrice.[76] Dante proved to trust Kaiser and both showed to care for each other, to the point that Dante was extremely shaken at the end of the 20th episode of Deconjuration knowing that Kaiser stayed behind.
  • Leonardo Gomes: Dante said he was very friendly with Leonardo, coming to save the life of the "friend" on the day of the fire at the St. Menefreda Orphanage. Both, with Jasmin, started together in the occultism after the fire. When asked about Leonardo’s real intentions, Dante always protected him saying he was a good person, reinforcing that his friend wanted to save Denis Batista in the ritual in which the occultist supposedly died. Dante, seeing Leonardo as Kian, is extremely enraged at Gal and the Escribes, showing that he really cared for him.
  • Luciano Carvalho: Luciano had extreme resentment and hatred for Dante, but the occultist showed no bad intentions against him, keeping quiet and letting the former military threaten him without retaliating. Because of this, the relationship between the two was very complicated at first, easing with time. Even if the two argued sometimes, Luciano showed no more hatred, treating the occultist as a teammate; that is, until the Final Day.
  • Rubens Naluti: Even though he has not interacted much with Rubens, Dante treats him well, pledging to help him as a teammate, and also trusts in his abilities to help his teammates, as seen in the 8th episode of Calamity, in which Balu was dying on the floor, and Dante starts screaming for Rubens to go help him save the man’s life.[77]
  • Mr. Verity: Initially, Verity treated Dante as just another occultist arrested at the base of the Order. However, at the request of the D-Force to release him from his cell, Verity trusted his agents and allowed such a situation.[78] After Dante helped the team investigate and fight the paranormal, Mr. Verity officially recruited the occultist for Ordo Realitas.[79] After the disastrous battle against Gal and Kian, Dante breaks down and questions whether they should continue to fight the Escribes.[72] Mr. Verity says he saw in Dante’s heart a resemblance to that of Arnaldo and claims to need someone like him by his side again. He consoles Dante and convinces the agent that they should fight together for those who have gone and avenge everyone they loved. After the events of Deconjuration, Dante becomes an increasingly important agent for the Order, and now, having great confidence on the part of Verity, he trusts Dante and his team for some of the most important tasks of the Ordo Realitas.

Trivia[]

  • Dante's name was inspired in the writer Dante Alighieri, author of "The Divine Comedy", being one of the favorite works of the character.[80]
  • Rakin said that Dante's clerical necklace is purely aesthetic and has no connection with creed itself. It is used by Dante to make him more reliable in the eyes of ordinary and religious people.
  • Dante often names his rituals in Italian, as it is the original language of his favorite work, "The Divine Comedy" by Dante Alighieri.[81]
  • According to Rakin, Dante did not consider anyone of the Order as friends in Deconjuration, since he had known them for a short time, and his greatest motivation to be allied with them was Beatrice.
  • One of Dante's goals was to destroy the blood creature created on the ritual involving a child and an Order agent.[82] In the "Prologue", he achieved this.
  • Dante has a tattoo on his back, being the Caduceus of Hermes. For Dante, the tattoo represents the duality between science and religion that the character always faces in his life and in his decisions.[83]
  • Rakin confirmed that when creating Dante, he thought of the exact opposite of Thiago: without charisma, intelligent and introverted.
  • Dante is allergic to peanuts.[84]
  • Dante, that grew up in the St. Menefreda Orphanage, was the one that gave the final blow to both Enpap-X[85] and Sukkalgir,[86] that were previously Father Ronaldo and Sister Thelma, the Orphanage caretakers.
  • Dante fulfilled the role of Tirigan on Leonardo Gomes' Deconjuration ritual.[4]
  • Rakin confirmed, after the revelation of his "Lusidii" form, that the black lines on Dante's face refers the crying and smiling faces commonly used in the ancient Greece to tragedy and comedy theaters. The marks also reference the old mark of Thiago Fritz in his Lusidii form, that formed a cross, as well as Dante’s when together.[87]
  • Dante’s original idea was to be a priest mixing religion and science. After a brief discussion, Cellbit and Rakin decided by a very studious Occultist, but keeping the appearance of the original idea.[88]
  • According to Rakin in one of his streams, Dante's Focused Energy Shot Ritual was inspired in one of the abilities of Yusuke Urameshi, the protagonist from Yu Yu Hakusho.
  • Dante has a habit of laughing in nervous situations. This can be observed when he confronted Gal in the Final Day of the Deconjuration,[32] during the discussion with Joui and also when he confronted The Magistrate about Arnaldo Fritz and the Relic of Energy.
  • When Dante was arrested at the base of the Order, he kept books hidden in his cell behind a loose stone from the wall, and read them secretly at night.[89]
  • Dante was the protagonist with more sanity in Paranormal Order, as seen in "Orphanage".[90]
  • Rakin's idea, during the transition of Deconjuration to Calamity, was to turn Dante into a Combatant. This was not possible due to the system change and that the group would be without an Occultist.[91]
  • Dante was abandoned in the orphanage as a baby, even before completing 1 year of life.
  • His appearance in Signals from the Other Side is inspired in the actor Keanu Reeves.[92]
  • According to Gabi and later confirmed by Rakin, Dante's suit was made by Carina.[93][94]
  • The suit worn by Dante in Signals from the Other Side has a specific reason for being worn, which has not yet been revealed by Rakin.[95]
